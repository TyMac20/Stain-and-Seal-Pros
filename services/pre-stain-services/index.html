Wood Pre-Stain Services in Springfield, MO

Protect every board on all four sides — before it's even installed.

Pre-Stain Service Photo – All 4 Sides

What Most People Don't Know

When a fence or deck is built the traditional way, only the exposed face gets stained. The backs, tops, and bottoms of the boards? Completely unprotected. Moisture wicks in from every hidden surface, and that's where warping, cracking, and rot start — from the inside out.

Pre-staining solves that problem. We coat every board on all four sides with premium stain and sealant before installation. The result is wood that's sealed from every angle and lasts dramatically longer than boards stained after the fact.

6 Benefits of Pre-Staining

All-Four-Sides Protection

Every surface is sealed — front, back, top, and bottom. No hidden wood left exposed to moisture.

Longer Wood Life

Pre-stained boards resist warping, cracking, and rot far longer than boards stained after installation.

Moisture Defense

Sealed end-cuts and hidden faces prevent moisture wicking — the #1 cause of premature wood failure.

Better Color Consistency

Boards stained flat and individually get more even color than boards stained after they're nailed up.

Saves Money Long-Term

A small upfront investment prevents expensive board replacements and early restaining down the road.

Professional Results

No drips, no runs, no missed spots. Each board gets full, even coverage in a controlled environment.

How Pre-Staining Works

1

Delivery or Drop-Off

Boards are delivered to our facility or we pick them up from your supplier. We coordinate timing with your build schedule.

2

Inspection & Prep

We inspect each board for defects and prep surfaces for optimal stain absorption. Any warped or damaged boards are flagged before staining.

3

All-Four-Sides Staining

Every board is coated on all four sides — front, back, top, and bottom — with premium stain and sealant. Full, even coverage from end to end.

4

Drying & Curing

Boards are racked and dried to full cure before handling or transport. This ensures a durable finish that won't transfer during installation.

5

Ready for Installation

Boards are stacked, labeled, and ready for your installer to pick up or for us to deliver to the job site.

Pre-Stain FAQs

Pre-staining means coating fence or deck boards on all four sides with stain and sealant before they're installed. Once boards are nailed up, you can only reach the exposed face — the backs, tops, and bottoms are hidden and unprotected. Pre-staining seals out moisture from every angle, which dramatically reduces warping, cracking, and rot.

We recommend waiting 24–48 hours for the stain to fully cure before handling and installation. This ensures the finish is durable and won't transfer or smudge during install. We'll coordinate the timing with your builder or installer so there's no delay in your project.

Minor scuffing can happen at nail points and where boards are handled, but the overall finish stays intact. After the fence or deck is up, we can do a quick touch-up pass on any marks. The important thing is that all four sides are sealed — the hidden surfaces are what matter most for long-term protection.

Any cuts made during installation expose bare wood. We recommend sealing cut ends with an end-cut preservative, which your installer can do on-site. We can also schedule a post-installation touch-up to seal any exposed cuts and ensure complete coverage.

Yes. We pre-stain lumber for fences, decks, pergolas, and any outdoor wood structure. If you're building it from raw lumber, we can coat it before assembly. This is especially valuable for deck joists and underside boards that are impossible to reach after the deck is built.

Pre-stain pricing depends on the number of boards, wood type, and stain product. It's typically a fraction of what you'd spend on premature board replacement due to rot. We provide exact quotes based on your project scope — just tell us what you're building and how much lumber is involved.
